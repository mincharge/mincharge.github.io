AC (slow) — amenity model

For residents and employees. Building pays MinCharge a fixed monthly rent plus an operating charge (e.g., ₹2/kWh). Residents are billed near the building’s electricity tariff. No profit intent for building owners—focus on being EV‑friendly.

DC (fast) — pegged tariff

For public‑facing, high‑turnover sites. Users pay a pegged ₹24/kWh. MinCharge receives ₹8/kWh; the remainder covers your power cost and potential margin.

FAQ

Common questions

Who pays for the equipment?

MinCharge invests the upfront capital for charging equipment, networking, and software. The building provides an electrical connection.

How is AC (slow) charging billed?

Residents are billed near the building’s electricity tariff (via app or RFID). The building pays MinCharge a fixed monthly rent plus an operating charge (e.g., ₹2/kWh). This is positioned as an amenity — no profit intent for building owners.

How is DC (fast) charging billed?

Users pay a pegged tariff of ₹24/kWh. MinCharge receives ₹8/kWh; the remainder covers your power cost and potential margin.

Do you support RFID without wallets?

Yes. We support both app‑based and RFID‑based access, with or without dedicated wallets—ideal for residents, guests, and fleets.

What hardware do you support?

We are hardware‑agnostic and OCPP‑native. We deploy AC and DC chargers matched to demand, with dynamic load management where needed.

Where have you deployed?

Across apartments, offices, and especially restaurants and business hubs—where our network and experience enable high availability and utilization.
